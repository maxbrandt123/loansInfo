How Loans Work

A loan is transfer of money from one party to another. Someone may ask for a loan when the need money, but don't have any. Someone may loan a needy person money for the prospect of collecting interest. Interest is a predetermined percent of the loaned money that is added on to the total the loanee owes the loaner.



Simple Interest Loans

When taking out a loan, the amount borrowed is referred to as the principal. A SIL (Simple Interest Loan) is where the interest was calculated by multiplying the principal by the rate, multiplied by the number of time periods.
A simple equation to find the SIL is i = P x r x t
interest = Principal x rate x time


Compound Interest Loan

A CIL (Compound Interest Loan) is a loan based off of the interest from the initial principle, as well as the the other interest charges of previous time periods. CIL can be properly explained as 'interest on the interest'. It makes the total $ owed grow very quickly.
Example: If I loan you $100 with a %10 monthly interest, after one month, you would owe me back $110 (10% of 100 is 10, so the interest you would owe me is $10. $10 plus the original $100 you took is a total of $110). However, after the second month, you know longer only owe me just an additional $10. You would now owe me 10% of the new total ($110), which is an additional $11 ($11 dollars is the product of $10 of the latest total, $110). The new total owed to me would be $121 ($110 + $11 = $121.)


Interest Only Loan

An IOL (Interest Only Loan) is basically a mortgage with an adjustable rate that allows the loanee just the interest rate for the first few years or months (this time period is predetermined by the loaner and loanee.) That "teaser rate" introductory period typically lasts one, three, or five years. The payment rises and lowers with the Libor rate (Libor stands for the London Interbank Offering Rate.) Libor is the rate banks/loaners charge eachother for small loans. As Libor rises, so does the interest payment. As Libor lowers, so does the interest payment.
